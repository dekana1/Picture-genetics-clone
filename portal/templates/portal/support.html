Our genes contain all the informathion we need to function as humans. Generally, we each inherit two copies of each gene: one form our mom, and one from our dad. Most of the genes look exactky the same from one person to another, no matter how closely related. The feww genetic variations that do exist from person to person are the ones that determine thi gs like the colour of our eyes, or whether we get freckles. Sometimes, the genes that make us different from other people are genes that increase our risk for certain diseases and health conditions. Such genes are often passed down from parents to children, or, occasionally, they arise spontaneaously. Genetic testing helps identify variations in genes that pose a potential health risk for you and your family.
